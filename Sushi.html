Ingredients

  • Sushi rice
  • Nori (seaweed sheets)
  • Fresh fish (such as tuna, salmon, or yellowtail), or other fillings like cucumber, avocado, or cooked shrimp
  • Rice vinegar
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Soy sauce
  • Wasabi
  • Pickled ginger (optional)

Equipment:

  • Bamboo sushi rolling mat (makisu)
  • Sharp knife
  • Rice cooker or pot for cooking rice
  • Wooden sushi rice paddle (or a spatula)

Process

  1. Prepare the Sushi Rice:
    • Rinse 2 cups of sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
    • Cook the rice according to the package instructions, using slightly less water than usual to make the rice a bit firmer. Once cooked, let the rice cool slightly.
    • Season the cooked rice with a mixture of 1/4 cup of rice vinegar, 2 tablespoons of sugar, and 1 teaspoon of salt. Gently fold to incorporate the seasoning.
  2. Prepare the Fillings:
    • Slice your desired fillings into thin strips. This can include fish, vegetables, or cooked shrimp.
  3. Assemble the Sushi Rolls:
    • Lay a sheet of nori shiny side down on a bamboo sushi rolling mat.
    • Spread a thin layer of sushi rice evenly over the nori, leaving about 1 inch of space at the top.
    • Arrange your fillings in a line across the center of the rice.
    • Roll the sushi tightly, starting from the bottom and rolling away from you. Apply gentle pressure to seal the roll.
  4. Slice and Serve:
    • Use a sharp knife to slice the sushi roll into bite-sized pieces. Wet the knife with water between cuts to prevent sticking.
    • Arrange the sushi pieces on a serving plate and serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.
