Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, potatoes, etc.), chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind pulp
  • 3 tablespoons bisi bele bath powder (available in stores or homemade)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Chopped coriander leaves for garnish
  • For tempering (tadka):
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Curry leaves

Process

  1. Wash the rice and toor dal separately. Soak the rice for about 15-20 minutes
  2. In a pressure cooker, cook the soaked rice and toor dal with enough water until they are soft and mushy. You can also cook them in a pot, but it will take longer
  3. In a separate pan, heat ghee or oil for tempering
  4. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then add cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves
  5. Add chopped onions to the pan and sauté until they turn translucent
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy
  7. Add the mixed vegetables to the pan and sauté for a few minutes
  8. Add tamarind pulp, bisi bele bath powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well
  9. Add water to the pan to achieve the desired consistency (similar to a thick soup). Bring it to a boil and let it simmer for a few minutes until the vegetables are cooked
  10. Once the vegetables are cooked, add the cooked rice and dal mixture to the pan. Mix everything together gently
  11. Adjust the seasoning if needed and let the bisi bele bath simmer for a few more minutes to blend the flavors
  12. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves
