Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice (you can also use any other long-grain rice)
  • 1 ripe mango, peeled and diced
  • 2 tablespoons oil (coconut oil or vegetable oil)
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 2-3 dry red chilies, broken into pieces
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons grated coconut (optional)
  • Chopped coriander leaves for garnish

Process

  1. Cook the rice using your preferred method (boiling, rice cooker, etc.) and let it cool down slightly
  2. In a pan, heat the oil over medium heat
  3. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter
  4. Add urad dal, chana dal, broken red chilies, and a pinch of asafoetida. Sauté until the dals turn golden brown
  5. Add turmeric powder and fresh curry leaves to the pan. Stir well
  6. Add the diced mango pieces and sauté for a couple of minutes until the mango softens slightly
  7. Add salt to taste and mix everything well
  8. Turn off the heat and add the cooked rice to the pan. Gently mix the rice with the mango mixture until well combined
  9. Optionally, add grated coconut to the mango rice for extra flavor and texture
  10. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves before serving
  11. Mango rice is best enjoyed fresh and can be served as a main dish or as a side dish with yogurt or a pickle
