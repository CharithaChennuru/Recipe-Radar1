Ingredients

  • 500 grams of minced mutton (you can also use lamb)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon of red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon of garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying the koftas

    • Ingredients for Curry:

  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon of red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing

Process

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the minced mutton, chopped onion, green chilies, coriander leaves, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, garam masala powder, and salt. Mix everything well and shape the mixture into small round balls (koftas).
  2. Heat oil in a pan and fry the koftas until they are golden brown on all sides. Remove them from the oil and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add more oil or ghee if needed. Add the chopped onion and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  6. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala powder, and salt. Mix well and cook the masala for a few minutes.
  7. Add water as needed to adjust the consistency of the curry.
  8. Gently place the fried koftas into the curry and simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.
